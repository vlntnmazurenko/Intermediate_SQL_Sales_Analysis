# Intermediate_SQL_Sales_Analysis
## Overview
This project was completed as part of an SQL course by [Luke Barousse](https://www.youtube.com/@LukeBarousse). It focuses on analyzing customer behavior in an e-commerce business to better understand customer value, purchasing patterns and retention. The analysis demonstrates how SQL can be used to support data-driven business decisions and customer growth strategies.  
## Business Questions
The project explores the following business questions:
- Customer Segmentation: Which customers contribute the most value to the business?
- Cohort Analysis: How does revenue differ across customer cohorts over time?
- Retention Analysis: Which customers are at risk of churning based on their recent purchasing activity?
## Analysis Approach
### 1. Customer Segmentation Analysis
- Calculated each customer's Lifetime Value (LTV) using historical purchase data.
- Grouped customers into High, Mid, and Low value segments based on their LTV.
- Measured key business metrics, including total revenue generated by each customer segment.  

Query: [1_customer_segmentation.sql](https://github.com/vlntnmazurenko/Intermediate_SQL_Sales_Analysis/blob/main/1_customer_segmentation.sql)

### Key Findings
- High-Value customers generate the majority of revenue, contributing 65.6% of the total LTV while representing only 25% of the customer base.
- Mid-Value customers account for 32.3% of total LTV and make up 50% of all customers, making them the largest customer segment.
- Low-Value customers represent 25% of customers but contribute only 2.1% of total LTV, indicating a significant gap in customer value across segments.
### Business Insights
- Prioritize retention efforts for High-Value customers, as losing even a small number of them could have a substantial impact on overall revenue.
- Develop targeted campaigns to move Mid-Value customers into the High-Value segment, since they represent the largest pool with strong growth potential.
- Optimize acquisition and engagement strategies for Low-Value customers to improve profitability while avoiding excessive investment in users with limited lifetime value.

### 2. Cohort Analysis
- Organized customers into cohorts based on the year of their first purchase.
- Evaluated the number of customers and total revenue generated by each cohort.
- Compared cohort performance to understand long-term customer retention and revenue trends.  

Query: [2_cohort_analysis.sql](https://github.com/vlntnmazurenko/Intermediate_SQL_Sales_Analysis/blob/main/2_cohort_analysis.sql)

### Key Findings 
- Revenue generally increased alongside customer acquisition from 2015 to 2019, with both metrics peaking before a noticeable decline in 2020.
- The 2022 cohort achieved the highest customer acquisition (9,010 customers) while generating the second-highest total revenue ($21.5M), indicating strong acquisition performance.
- The 2024 cohort shows substantially lower revenue and customer counts than previous years, likely because it represents a more recent cohort with limited time to generate lifetime revenue.
### Business Insights
- Replicate the acquisition strategies used in 2018–2022, as these cohorts consistently delivered both high customer volumes and strong revenue.
- Investigate the sharp decline in the 2020 cohort to determine whether external factors, market conditions, or operational changes affected customer acquisition and spending.
- Continue monitoring recent cohorts (2023–2024) before drawing long-term conclusions, as their lifetime value is still developing and may increase over time.
